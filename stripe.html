Stripe Suits

Welcome back to our second Mahjong class!

Today, we're going to learn about the second type of mahjong tile: the stripe.

Just like the "circle" tiles, the stripe tiles also come in various styles.

They're originally inspired by bamboo sticks, or you can think of them as vertical lines.

Different numbers of stripes make up a deck of cards representing different numbers.

Like the circle tiles we learned about last time, stripe tiles represent numbers from one to nine, with a total of nine tiles.

However, the one stripe tile is a bit special. Often, it is depicted with a bird.

So, when you see tiles with birds like peacocks, chickens, or swallows on them, those represent the one stripe.

This is typically what the stripe tiles look like, from one to nine.

And, just like in playing cards, each type of stripe tile comes in four identical pieces.

Thus, there are a total of 36 stripe tiles.

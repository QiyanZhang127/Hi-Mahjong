What is Mahjong?

Mahjong is a traditional tile-based game that originated in China and is widely popular around the world. A standard set of mahjong tiles consists of 136 tiles, including three suits: dots, bamboos, and characters, each ranging from one to nine with four tiles of each, totaling 108 tiles. There are also wind tiles (East, South, West, North) and dragon tiles (Red, Green, White), with four tiles of each, totaling 28 tiles.

Mahjong is usually played by four people, each holding 13 tiles. At the start of the game, the dealer (the first person to deal the tiles) takes one extra tile. Then, in a counterclockwise direction, each player takes turns drawing a tile from the wall and discarding an unwanted tile, a process called "draw and discard." When a player discards a tile that another player needs, they can choose to "chow," "pong," or "kong" the tile.

The objective of the game is to form specific tile combinations, by drawing and discarding tiles according to the rules, ultimately achieving a winning hand (mahjong). The first player to achieve a winning hand is the winner, and the game ends.
